Account Registration & Email Setup

New employee onboarding guide

Welcome to Gresham! We are thrilled to be able to support you through your onboarding process. This guide will walk you through the steps required to access and set up your greshamtech.com email address — from logging in for the first time through to setting up Multifactor Authentication — ensuring you can access core Gresham resources.

Overview

We'll progress through this document using the following steps. It's all relatively straightforward, and the Gresham IT team will be on hand to help with any issues. If you need to contact us, email itonboarding@greshamtech.com and we'll be happy to help.

  1. First time account sign in
  2. Multifactor authentication setup
  3. Temporary password reset
  4. Accepting the Gresham AUP (Acceptable Use Policy)
1

Receiving your account details

Our IT team will send an email to your spglobal.com email address with the information needed to log in to Gresham systems. Once you receive that email, you can proceed with setting up your account.

Your Gresham email address will generally follow the same format as your SPGlobal address. For example, if your name is Jane Doe, your address will be jane.doe@greshamtech.com.

You will also receive a temporary password. This is not your final password — you will be prompted to change it as part of the onboarding process.

Password requirements
  • Must be at least 8 characters
  • Must contain upper and lower case characters
  • Must contain numbers and/or symbols
2

Signing in with your email and temporary password

Open a web browser and navigate to https://www.office365.com.

If you are already signed in with your SPGlobal account, click on your profile picture in the top right of the screen and choose "Sign in with a different account".

When the Microsoft dialog appears, choose "Use another account".

On the sign-in dialog, enter your Gresham email address in the format firstname.surname@greshamtech.com.

Click Next and then enter the temporary password that was issued to you.

3

Setting up Microsoft Authenticator for MFA

Once you have logged in with your temporary password, you'll be prompted to set up additional security. Click Next on that dialog.

You will need to have Microsoft Authenticator installed on your phone — you may already have this for your SPGlobal login.

Click Next on the Install Microsoft Authenticator screen, then click Next again to display the QR code.

On the Microsoft Authenticator app on your phone, tap the + symbol in the top right, select Work or School account, then Scan QR code. Scan the code on screen — you will see a new entry in Authenticator for Gresham Technologies.

Click Next and you'll receive a test notification to your mobile phone. Enter the number displayed on screen.

Once you've approved the notification, click Close on the Authenticator Added screen.

You'll then be prompted to set up your mobile number for SMS recovery codes. Enter your mobile number with the country code and click Next. Enter the code you receive by text message.

4

Change your temporary password

Once you have completed MFA setup, you will be prompted to change your password.

Enter your temporary password, then enter a new password and confirm it. Refer to the password requirements listed in Step 1.

5

Accept the Gresham AUP (Acceptable Use Policy)

Once you have updated your password, you'll be prompted to read through and accept the Gresham AUP.

Please make sure you read all the way to the bottom of the document before clicking the Accept button.

Once this process is complete, you will be registered with Gresham and will have access to the various resources that will be shared with you over the coming weeks and months.

Questions? Contact the IT onboarding team at itonboarding@greshamtech.com
